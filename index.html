Mission — Practical Oversight for Intelligent Systems

AURORA develops governance architectures and in-system supervisory layers designed to detect, classify and mitigate AI-driven threats in high-impact domains (financial systems, government services and critical digital infrastructure). The project emphasizes transparency, auditability and human-in-the-loop control to ensure accountability and safety in deployed intelligent systems.

Key priorities

  • Embedded supervisory layer that monitors decision-flows and raises contextual alerts
  • Transparent and auditable logging for explainability and incident review
  • Threat classification and graded response policies tailored to domain risk
  • Human-in-the-loop oversight and escalation procedures

Research Overview

This research proposes a layered governance architecture that is embedded within intelligent operating environments to provide continuous oversight. The architecture separates responsibilities into clear layers (monitoring, detection, adjudication, human escalation) and uses explainable signals so operators can understand both the cause and context of alerts.

Practical contributions

  • Design of in-system monitoring agents that capture decision provenance.
  • Threat taxonomy for AI-enabled attacks affecting finance and public services.
  • Policy engine for graded countermeasures and controlled interruption.
  • Audit flows and incident reproducibility for accountable review.

Why this matters

As intelligent systems take operational roles in sensitive sectors, conventional perimeter security is insufficient. Governance must be embedded, observable and actionable — enabling organizations to respond quickly while preserving due process and legal safeguards.
